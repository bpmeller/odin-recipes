Ingredients

  • One can of garbanzo beans (chickpeas)
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ cup of tahini (also known as tehina)
  • Fresh parsley (optional for serving)

Steps

  1. Place garbanzo beans in a small saucepan or pot, add 1 teaspoon baking soda (this alters the pH to encourage the beans to break down and shed their skins), and cover with a few inches of water. Bring to a boil and cook for 20 minutes, until the skins begin to fall off and the beans look overcooked/mushy. You may need to reduce the heat after a few minutes to prevent boil-overs.
  2. While the garbanzo beans are boiling, combine the garlic and lemon juice with ¼ teaspoon salt in the workbowl of your food processor. Pulse until a fine, uniform consistency is achieved. You may need to scrape down the top and sides of the bowl to ensure the garlic is chopped finely enough. Leave the mixture for at least 10 minutes for the raw garlic taste to mellow.
  3. Continue to process the hummus on high for at least one minute longer, up to three minutes longer if it hasn’t become smooth enough yet. You can make the hummus thinner at this point by slowly adding ice water a teaspoon at a time while processing on high. Taste the hummus at this point to determine if it needs more lemon juice or more salt for flavor.
  4. Serve immediately or transfer to an airtight container and store in the refrigerator for up to one week.
